Type of Tea Description How to make?
SILVER TIPS Silver needle white tea/Ceylon Silver Tips, commonly known as White Tea/Silver needle, is a very rare and expensive Ceylon tea in Sri Lanka. Silver Tips is entirely handmade tea. A small team of experienced pickers handpicks just the buds from select bushes. Use fresh, cool, oxygenated water.Generally, brewing white tea requires hot water at 175º (simply add two ice cubes for every 8 oz of boiling water to achieve this temperature). If the water is too hot, the tea will be scalded, causing it to become bitter and astringent. Please allow us to steep for 4-7 minutes. Then remove the tea leaves. Add sweetener/milk according to your own preferences.
OP – Orange Pekoe Contrary to popular belief, Orange Pekoe refers to a leaf size and not a flavour. Orange Pekoe tea has a strong scent and a smooth flavour with a sweet finish. Makes sure that fresh, pure, cold-filtered water is used. Firstly, boil the water. If using store-bought tea bags, simply add a tea bag to a mug and fill it with the hot water. If using loose leaf tea, use 2–3 grams of tea leaves for every six ounces(3-4 cups) of water in a strainer. Let the tea steep in the water for 3–5 minutes, depending on your taste preference. For a stronger tea, use more tea leaves and steep for a longer period. After steeping, remove the tea leaves or tea bag from the water and enjoy it.
DUST DUST tea is the smallest grade of tea in Sri Lanka. It is much finer than fannings(BOPF) which is often used in teabags. Further, we use DUST tea grade to make very strong black tea blending with similar tea grades. For example, this tea grade is perfect for milk tea blends such as Hongkong Milk tea. In Sri Lanka, all our tea factories produce DUST tea grades in all regions. However, low grown dust tea makes a strong, robust, and bitter taste for your cup. Use fresh, cool, oxygenated water. pre-heated or hot tap water. Heat the water and pour it directly over the leaves. (Use about one teaspoon of tea per cup.) For black tea, allow the water to come to a gentle boil. Steep the tea in an infuser about four to five minutes for black tea, Then remove the tea leaves. Perfect tea is brewed one cup at a time. Personal tastes, vary, of course — you’ll want to adjust the measurements and brewing times according to your own preferences. Add sweetener/milk according to your own preferences.
GOLDEN TIPS Ceylon Golden Tips tea goes into the white tea category. This product has a unique taste and very smooth to drink. This tea is an exclusive very rare tea that people believe has great value. so per kilogram are significantly higher than other teas. Use fresh, cool, oxygenated water. Generally, brewing of white tea requires hot water at 175º (simply add two ice cubes for every 8 oz of boiling water to achieve this temperature). If the water is too hot, the tea will be scalded, causing it to become bitter and astringent. Please allow us to steep for 4-7 minutes. Then remove the white tea leaves. Add sweetener/milk according to your own preferences
GUNPOWDER This green tea dates back to the 1800s and the name originates from the fact that the tea is rolled very tightly into small pellets befitting ammunition used in old muskets. Use fresh, cool, oxygenated water.Generally, brewing white tea requires hot water at 175º. Please allow to steep for 1-2 minutes. Then remove the Green tea leaves.Add sweetener or few Lemon drops to your own preferences
